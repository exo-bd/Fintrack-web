Introduction

FinTrack ("FinTrack", "we", "our", "us") is a personal finance app for Android, developed under the package com.fintrack.app. This policy explains what information the app accesses, why, and how it is stored. It applies to anyone who installs or uses FinTrack.

FinTrack is built around a simple idea: your financial data should help only you. Wherever possible, information is processed on your device rather than sent anywhere.

Information we collect

FinTrack collects only what it needs to run your ledger:

  • Account information — your name, email address, and profile photo, when you sign in with Google or create an account, via Firebase Authentication.
  • Financial data you create — transactions, budgets, categories, debts, recurring bills, and notes, whether entered manually, detected from SMS, or scanned from a receipt.
  • SMS content — read on-device to detect bank and mobile-banking transaction alerts. FinTrack extracts the amount, date, and merchant/sender pattern needed to create a ledger entry; it does not upload or store the raw message text.
  • Camera and photos — receipt images you choose to scan or photos you choose to attach, processed to extract amounts and dates.
  • Voice input — audio captured only while you actively use voice entry to add a transaction by speaking; it is converted to text on-device and not retained as an audio file.
  • Biometric data — handled entirely by your device's operating system to unlock the app. FinTrack never receives or stores your fingerprint or face data.
  • Gamification data — points, streaks, and reward progress generated from your in-app activity.
  • Device & diagnostic data — basic technical information (device model, OS version, crash logs) used to keep the app stable.

How we use your information

  • To create and maintain your transaction ledger, budgets, debts, and recurring bills.
  • To sync your data securely across your own devices when you're signed in.
  • To send budget alerts, bill reminders, and reward notifications you've enabled.
  • To detect and prevent fraud, abuse, or technical issues.
  • To improve app stability and performance.
FinTrack does not use your financial data, SMS content, or receipt images to build an advertising profile, and does not sell this data to anyone.

Permissions we request

Android requires apps to declare each permission up front. Here is what FinTrack asks for and why:

PermissionWhy FinTrack needs it
READ_SMS / RECEIVE_SMSDetect and auto-categorize transactions from bank and mobile-banking alerts.
CAMERAScan paper receipts and, optionally, set a profile photo.
READ_MEDIA_IMAGES / VIDEO / AUDIOAttach existing photos, such as a saved receipt, from your gallery.
RECORD_AUDIOAdd a transaction by speaking, using on-device speech-to-text.
USE_BIOMETRICLock the app behind your fingerprint or face unlock.
POST_NOTIFICATIONSSend budget alerts, bill reminders, and reward notifications.
SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARMTrigger reminders and recurring-bill alerts at the exact time you set.
RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETEDRestore your scheduled reminders after your device restarts.
FOREGROUND_SERVICE / FOREGROUND_SERVICE_DATA_SYNCKeep data sync running reliably while the app is open.
INTERNETSign in, sync your ledger to your account, and process premium purchases.
WAKE_LOCK / VIBRATEBriefly keep the screen awake during sync and give haptic feedback for reminders.

You can review or revoke any of these at any time from Android Settings → Apps → FinTrack → Permissions. Revoking a permission only disables the related feature; it does not delete data you've already saved.

Storage & security

  • Local-first: your ledger is stored on your device first, in an encrypted local database, before anything syncs to the cloud.
  • Cloud sync: when signed in, data syncs to Cloud Firestore under your own Firebase account, governed by per-account access rules so one account can never read another's data.
  • Sensitive fields: credentials and tokens are kept in encrypted secure storage provided by the Android operating system, not in plain text.
  • Account isolation: on a shared device, switching accounts does not expose another account's transactions, points, or settings.

Third-party services

FinTrack relies on a small set of trusted providers to operate:

  • Google Firebase (Authentication, Cloud Firestore, Cloud Messaging) — account sign-in, data sync, and push notifications.
  • Google Sign-In — optional one-tap sign-in using your Google account.
  • Google AdMob — displays ads that help keep the free tier of FinTrack running.
  • Google Play Billing — processes premium subscription purchases; FinTrack never sees your card details.

Each provider processes data under its own privacy terms in addition to this policy.

Advertising

FinTrack shows ads through Google AdMob on free-tier screens. Depending on your region and consent choices, ads may be personalized or non-personalized. You can manage ad personalization in your device's Google Settings, and remove ads entirely with a premium subscription.

Data sharing & disclosure

We do not sell your personal or financial data. We share data only with the service providers listed above, as needed to operate the app, or when required by law.

Data retention & deletion

Your data is retained for as long as your account is active. You can delete individual entries at any time inside the app. To request full account and data deletion, contact us using the details below — we will process verified requests within a reasonable timeframe.

Children's privacy

FinTrack is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from them. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us so we can remove it.

Your choices & rights

  • Revoke any Android permission at any time from system settings.
  • Disable SMS-based detection and enter transactions manually instead.
  • Turn off notifications, reminders, or ad personalization independently.
  • Request a copy or deletion of your data by contacting us.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as FinTrack evolves. Material changes will be reflected by updating the effective date at the top of this page.
